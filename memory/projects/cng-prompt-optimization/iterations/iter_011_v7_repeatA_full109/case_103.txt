Impression
Jacqueline Philippe presents with a complex and multifaceted clinical picture characterized by chronic pain, recurrent gastrointestinal symptoms, and severe allergic reactions. Her thoracic spine MRI confirms a small disc herniation at T8-T9, which may contribute to her persistent back pain, though it does not explain her widespread symptoms. The lack of improvement with gabapentin and the presence of nausea, vomiting, and recurrent angioedema episodes suggest a possible underlying autoimmune or inflammatory process, despite normal ACE levels and negative lymph node biopsies for granulomatous disease.
