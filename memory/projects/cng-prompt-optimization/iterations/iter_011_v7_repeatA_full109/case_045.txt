Impression
Mr. Nickerson has a concerning pulmonary nodule in the right lower lobe with high metabolic activity on PET CT, raising suspicion for primary lung cancer. While no evidence of metastatic disease is present, the nodule’s size, morphology, and smoking history warrant further evaluation. The patient’s past medical history of Warthin’s tumor and vascular abnormalities, including iliac artery occlusion, adds complexity to his care. Further diagnostic steps are required to determine the nature of the nodule and guide appropriate management.

Plan
1. I will review the PET CT and head CT scan results with the patient to discuss the findings and implications of the pulmonary nodule.
2. We will arrange for a multidisciplinary team discussion involving pulmonology and thoracic surgery to determine the most appropriate next steps, which may include tissue sampling via bronchoscopy or transthoracic needle aspiration.
3. I will continue to support smoking cessation efforts and refer the patient to a smoking cessation program if he has not already been engaged.
4. We will follow up in approximately two months to review the results of the multidisciplinary discussion and any additional investigations or interventions that may be required.
