History of Present Illness
For the past year and a half, Irving has been experiencing thick green phlegm in his throat, which has worsened recently to the point where it causes nocturnal choking and panic attacks. He describes this phlegm as thick and difficult to dislodge, often requiring him to cough or use his inhalers to bring it up. He has noticed hoarseness of voice and bad breath in the mornings. His symptoms are exacerbated indoors, with better breathing when outside. Impression
Irving’s symptoms of thick green phlegm, nocturnal choking, and shortness of breath are most consistent with reflux-induced bronchial asthma. His chronic acid reflux, exacerbated by poor dietary habits and lack of bed elevation, is likely contributing to airway irritation, inflammation, and recurrent infections. The presence of hoarseness and morning bad breath further supports this diagnosis. While his pulmonary function tests show a restrictive and obstructive pattern, the primary driver of his symptoms appears to be gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) rather than a primary pulmonary process.

Plan
1. I will discontinue Anoro Ellipta and Teva-salbutamol HFA inhalers.
2. I will prescribe Inhale 200/5 mcg inhalation 2 puffs twice daily for maintenance, with instructions to use additional puffs as needed for symptom relief.
3. I will prescribe Jamp-nystatin 100,000 units/ml swish and swallow 5 ml four times daily for 2 weeks for oral candidiasis prophylaxis.
4. I will provide Irving with an anti-reflux diet sheet and instruct him to adhere to it strictly.
5. I will instruct Irving to elevate the head of his bed by at least 6 inches using a wedge pillow or similar device.
6. I will advise Irving to avoid eating or drinking for at least 3 hours before bedtime.
7. I will schedule a follow-up appointment in four months to reassess symptoms and response to treatment.
