Impression
Ms. Miller is a 73-year-old female with complex cardiovascular disease, including recent NSTEMIs, ICD placement, and heart failure with reduced ejection fraction, presenting for preoperative assessment prior to breast surgery. Her most recent MI in December 2025 places her within the high-risk perioperative window, compounded by bilateral lower extremity pain (likely vascular in origin) and poorly controlled diabetes. The presence of an ICD and multiple comorbidities further elevates her surgical risk, necessitating careful perioperative cardiac management and potential delay of elective surgery until at least 6 months post-MI if feasible.

Plan
1. We will confirm the timing of the December 2025 MI and, if within the last 3 months, delay elective breast surgery until at least 6 months post-MI to reduce perioperative cardiac risk.
2. We will schedule preoperative ICD interrogation and coordinate with cardiology and anesthesia to reprogram the device to asynchronous mode during surgery, with reactivation planned postoperatively.
3. We will arrange preoperative stress testing (e.g., stress echocardiogram) to assess myocardial viability and ischemic burden, with results guiding further management decisions.
4. We will consult vascular surgery to evaluate the bilateral lower extremity pain and assess for peripheral arterial disease, given her extensive vascular risk profile.
5. We will coordinate with cardiology to determine safe timing for discontinuation of ticagrelor (5-7 days preoperatively) while continuing aspirin, with consideration for bridging therapy if indicated.
6. We will discontinue metformin preoperatively and optimize heart failure and diabetes management, with close monitoring of electrolytes and renal function.
7. We will schedule follow-up within 2 months to reassess cardiac status, review stress test results, and finalize surgical planning.
